View from the Terrace

I wrote last week about the adventure of living abroad only beginning, and I am happy to report that just last weekend I spent three days in the uncharted territory that is Galicia. Famous for the Camino de Santiago and the Tower of Hercules in A Coruna, I was soon to learn that the region had plenty more to offer besides.

My own reason for the trip was to take part in the Iberian Gaelic Football Tournament, held in A Coruna, with Costa Gaels and Éire Óg Seville forming an Andalusian selection. Suffice to say we didn’t let our footballing commitments get in the way of having a good time, although the tournament itself was one of the many highlights of the weekend.

We acquitted ourselves well, only losing to the eventual winners Fillos de Breogan in the quarter-final, and we witnessed first-hand the commitment of the Galicians to the sport. The tournament was well-organised and we were treated with typical Galician hospitality (except when we were playing against them!) both on the day and the night that followed. Everyone involved deserves congratulations for their tireless promotion to the sport. That there were twelve men’s teams and nine ladies teams taking part is testament to both the appeal of the sport internationally and the people who are happy to act as its missionaries!

While the matches provided thrills and spills galore, there was much more to be gained from the weekend. We were lucky enough to spend Friday evening in Santiago itself, which truly lived up to its reputation. While I feel it is unfair to compare one place to another (if I did I imagine I’d never have left Cork!) it was a pleasure to be in surroundings that lacked any pretensions, where good food and a good time were high on the list of priorities. If the Costa del Sol is constantly trying to prove its worth and value at the same time, Galicia is much happier just as it is thanks very much. This in itself was refreshing.

The town of Santiago is nothing if not welcoming, and we were quick to take full advantage. We walked through cobbled streets where the architecture was more akin to Munich than Malaga, stopping off at eateries and wineries at our leisure. The drop in temperature was a small price to pay for a great night out.

On Saturday night we enjoyed a meal with the other teams from the tournament. Again, we were plied with food and drink, and after the presentations we were treated to the unique experience of a Spanish ska band. It worked pretty well too in case you’re wondering.

I won’t embarrass any of my team-mates by going into the specifics of Friday and Saturday night, but needless to say Sunday was taken as a day of rest and recuperation! Thankfully we were in just the place to do it.

All in all, the weekend was an unqualified success, even if it didn’t feel like that on Monday morning. It is a shame that most people’s perceptions of Spain start and finish on the Costa del Sol – if I learned anything this weekend it is that this country has much more going for it. I can’t wait to find out what else, and rest assured that you’ll be the first to know.
